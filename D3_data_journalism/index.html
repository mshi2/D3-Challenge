Correlations Between Level of Poverty and Healthcare

The data set included in this analysis is based on 2014 ACS 1-year estimates from the US Census Bureau.

The data shows a positive correlation between poverty and lack of healthcare. The states that cluster in the high poverty and higher lack of healthcare are southern states such as Mississippi, Louisiana, and Georgia. States with low levels of poverty such as Massachusetts, Vermont have higher level of healthcare. With regards to lack of healthcare, Texas is the most outstanding with 24% of the population lacking healthcare. Massachusetts is on the lower spectrum of lack of healthcare with 4.6% of the population lacking healthcare. This high level of healthcare in Massachusetts may be attributed to the near-universal healthcare insurance coverage stemming from the healthcare reform in 2006.
